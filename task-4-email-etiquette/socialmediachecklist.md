
Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

CSE0001 – Digital Literacy | Task 4 Part B| UJJWAL ANAND |25BCE10966

Do's

Keep professional profiles updated — LinkedIn and GitHub should reflect your current skills, projects, and education at all times. Think before you post — Ask yourself whether you would be comfortable with a future employer, professor, or family member seeing the content. Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) — Add an extra layer of security to all accounts, especially email and banking apps. Give credit for others' work — If you share someone's code, writing, or idea, always attribute the original creator. Use platforms purposefully — Use GitHub and Kaggle to document your learning publicly; let your online presence tell a story of growth.

Don'ts

Don't share personal identifiers publicly — Avoid posting your college ID number, phone number, home address, or daily schedule on any public platform. Don't spread unverified information — Do not forward news, screenshots, or claims without verifying them from a credible source first. Don't reuse passwords — Using the same password across multiple platforms means one breach exposes everything. Don't accept unknown connection requests blindly — Verify the identity of anyone you connect with, especially on LinkedIn and GitHub. Don't post grievances publicly — Complaints about professors, classmates, exams, or your institution should never appear on public social media. Use official channels instead.
