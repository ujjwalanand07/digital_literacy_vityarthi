General Online Safety  

Use strong, unique passwords** for every account — a password manager like Bitwarden can help you manage them without memorising each one.
Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)** on all important accounts — email, social media, and college portals.
Never click links in unsolicited emails or SMS messages** — go directly to the official website by typing the URL yourself.
Keep your devices and apps updated** — security patches fix known vulnerabilities that attackers actively exploit.
Do not share OTPs with anyone** — no bank, government body, or legitimate company will ever ask for your OTP over a call or message.
Check website URLs carefully** before entering login credentials — look for https:// and verify the domain spelling (e.g., sbi.co.in not sbi-login.net).
Financial / UPI Safety (Mandatory)  

Scanning a QR code always means you are paying money, never receiving it** — if someone says "scan this to receive your payment," it is a scam.
Never approve a UPI collect request from an unknown number** — a collect request is a demand for money from your account, not a transfer to you.
Reporting Cybercrime in India  

National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:cybercrime.gov.in Cyber Crime Helpline:1930 (available 24x7)  

Report immediately if you suspect fraud — early reporting increases the chance of freezing the fraudulent transaction before the money is moved.
